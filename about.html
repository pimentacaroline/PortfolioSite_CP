About

My Story

A videographer and a UX designer walk into a bar

My story starts in Brasil, where I was born and grew up. After finishing high school, I moved to Portugal, seeking better life opportunities. In Portugal, I worked for nine years as a saleswoman in a mall, where I learned a lot about people's needs and behaviors. Meanwhile, I studied Communication Science at Lisbon University, which opened the door for my first professional transition. Right after my graduation, I worked as a journalist in a TV program, and two years later, I launched myself as a videographer freelancer. As I use to say, being a videographer is a “one-woman show”. I was responsible for prospecting and relating with my clients, produce the videos (scriptwriting, shooting, interview, and edit), and when the "show" was too big, assembling and coordinating a team of coworkers. The videos that I produced in the last ten years, were mainly small documentaries about social innovation projects. Yes, I know, filming weddings are much more profitable, but my heart was always in Human Development subjects.

Coming back to our plot… the videographer is already at the “bar” and now will enter the UX Designer. So one day I was looking for remote work in a platform, and vacancies for UX designer was popping up all the time. UX Designer? What is that? It took only 3 hours reading about it to be sure that I found the perfect glove for my "professional being". I felt instantly attracted by the variety of disciplines, tools, and methodologies involved in the UX Design process. Still, mainly, I fell in love because it has humans at its core. I felt the call for another career change and found the perfect course at CareerFoundry. In one year, I got here, a freshly baked Ux Designer.

If you walked into the bar right now, you would see not just the videographer and the UX Designer, but also a shy wanderlust woman that traveled less than she wanted, an uninhibited Forró's dancer, the most awkward person at funerals, and the weirdo that can’t close a book after starting it because she needs to know how it ends (by the way, I strongly believe that curiosity, somehow, will be my cause of death).

Thank you for getting here. I love to hear other’s people stories, so please, get and touch and let’s talk.
